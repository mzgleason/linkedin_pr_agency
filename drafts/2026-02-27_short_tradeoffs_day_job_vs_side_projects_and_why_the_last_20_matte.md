# Tradeoffs: day job vs side projects and why the last 20% matters

This week the hardest decision was prioritizing work tied to my day job versus curiosities driven by these side projects. The signal I keep returning to is tradeoff clarity: list the risks, define the acceptance test, and then be willing to pause if the task no longer meets that bar.

A practical habit that helps: treat each automation as a feature with one measurable success criterion. In prior initiatives that focus yielded concrete business outcomes — for example, driving ~$50K/month from a new approval score model — because we shipped small, validated increments rather than a large, vague promise.

What single acceptance test will you use to decide whether a small project is worth finishing this week?
