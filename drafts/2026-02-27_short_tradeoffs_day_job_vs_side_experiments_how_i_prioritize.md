# Tradeoffs: day job vs side experiments (how I prioritize)

The hardest decision this week was splitting attention between my day job and experimental side projects. My approach: treat side work as tightly scoped learning experiments with clear success metrics and fixed timeboxes. If a project shows signal, allocate more time; if not, shelve it.

Practically that means aligning experiments to transferable learning (e.g., agent orchestration patterns, data validation techniques) so wins apply back to product work. That mindset has paid off before: recent product initiatives I led saved approximately $9M in annualized losses by resolving a security routing flaw and contributed to 200% YoY cross-sell growth. Those outcomes came from focused experiments that were then applied where they could scale.

How are you prioritizing attention between core responsibilities and exploratory work right now?
